# Oued Zenati: Between the Charm of the "Eloa" Statue and the Delight of Traditional Sweets

The city of Oued Zenati in Guelma Province is one of the regions that treasures historical secrets and a rich cultural heritage. In it, the fragrance of Roman and French history blends with the sweetness of authentic Algerian traditions, making it an exceptional stop for tourists seeking distinction.

![Old Mosque in the City](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-1.jpg)

## 1. The Legend of the "Eloa" Statue in Oued Zenati
The "Eloa" statue is one of the most controversial and curious landmarks in the region.

*   **Story and History:** The statue depicts a sad angel, inspired by a poem by the French poet Alfred de Vigny. It was placed during the French colonial era atop a monument to be a symbol of serenity and spirituality.
*   **Secret of Attraction:** Tourists are drawn to this statue not only for its artistic value but for the beauty of the surrounding site and the mountain vistas that Oued Zenati overlooks, making it an ideal spot for capturing exceptional souvenir photos.

![Eloa Statue in Oued Zenati](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-eloa.jpg)

## 2. The World of Traditional Sweets in Oued Zenati
A visit to Oued Zenati is incomplete without indulging in the experience of tasting the sweets for which the local markets are famous, reflecting the quality and generosity of the region's people:

### Zenati Zalabia
*   **Features:** The area's Zalabia is characterized by its golden color and a texture that is crispy on the outside and soft on the inside, covered in pure honey.
*   **Information for Tourists:** It is extensively sold in the old shops downtown; it is best enjoyed hot to taste its original flavor.

![Traditional Zalabia in Oued Zenati](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-zalabia.jpg)

### Kawkaw Candy (Peanut Candy)
*   **Features:** Oued Zenati is famous for making traditional "Kawkaw candy," where peanuts are roasted and mixed with caramel or honey to form hard, delicious pieces.
*   **Secret of Fame:** It is considered a favorite snack for travelers and tourists due to its ability to stay fresh for long periods.

### Roasted Nuts
*   **Diversity:** The markets offer a wide variety of almonds, walnuts, and pistachios roasted locally in traditional ways that preserve their benefits and strong flavor.
*   **Buying Tip:** Tourists can purchase assortments of mixed nuts as a wonderful souvenir from the region.

## 3. Al-Atiq Mosque
The Al-Atiq Mosque in the heart of Oued Zenati is a towering religious and historical monument, characterized by its authentic architectural style that reflects the region's identity. Beside it lies Independence Square (or the Central Square), the vibrant space where the city's people and visitors gather, considered an ideal place for rest and enjoying the surrounding architecture and quiet daily activities.

![Al-Atiq Mosque - Beacon of Knowledge](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-mosque-modern-old.jpg)


## The Scholar Amar Mehri: A Beacon of Knowledge and Reform in Oued Zenati
Oued Zenati in Guelma Province is considered one of the centers that hosted a pioneering scientific and reform movement, led by the scholar Amar Mehri, whose name became associated with the city's oldest mosque, "Al-Atiq Mosque," which today bears his name in his memory.

### His Upbringing and Scientific Education
Sheikh Amar Mehri was born in the El Harrouch area in 1887 and grew up in a mujahideen family; his grandfather "Abdullah" was among the companions of Sheikh El Mokrani and El Haddad in their glorious revolution. He memorized the Holy Quran at the school of Sheikh Ben Ali Sharif, then moved to Constantine to learn from great sheikhs such as Sheikh Ahmed Lounissi and Sheikh Sidi Ahmed El Habibatni. He was a classmate of the pioneer of the reformist renaissance, Sheikh Abdelhamid Ben Badis.

### Political Struggle and the National Movement
Sheikh Amar Mehri was not just a jurist but an outstanding political activist; he founded with Emir Khaled the nucleus of the first national movement. One of his most prominent historical positions was sending a petition of protest to the Governor-General against the decision of compulsory conscription for Algerians. He also dedicated his pen to serving the national cause through political articles and poems published in major newspapers of the time such as "Al-IÙ‚Ø¯Ø§Ù…", "Al-Farouq", and "Al-Najah".

### Settling in Oued Zenati and the Scientific Renaissance
In 1926, the scholar moved to Oued Zenati to be the imam and preacher of its Al-Atiq Mosque. From this pulpit, he launched a broad scientific movement based on the curricula of Ez-Zitouna University, turning the mosque into a beacon of knowledge from which scholars and judges graduated, such as Sheikh Mohamed El Rashidi, Sheikh Abdelrahman Belagoun, and Sheikh Tahar Boumediene.

![Old Al-Atiq Mosque](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-mosque-old-new.jpg)

### The Central Square and Al-Atiq Mosque: A Meeting of Knowledge and Beauty
Adjacent to Al-Atiq Mosque (currently Amar Mehri Mosque), Oued Zenati's Central Square stands as a witness to the city's history. While the mosque nourished minds with religion and knowledge, the surrounding square represented the city's beating heart, where families and visitors gather to feel the fragrance of the past and contemplate the ancient architecture that blends authenticity and history.

![Central Square in Oued Zenati](assets/img/blog/oued-zenati-square.jpg)

### The Scholar's Passing and His Great Funeral
Sheikh Amar Mehri died on April 19, 1933, and his passing had a profound impact on the hearts of the residents of Oued Zenati and all of Algeria. Shops were closed in mourning, and he was escorted in a solemn funeral attended by delegations from various parts of the country, leaving his name etched in the memory of the city and its Al-Atiq Mosque.
